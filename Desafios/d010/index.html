History of Android Mascot

Probably you now that the Android operational system, sustained by Google is one of the most used for mobile devices in all world. But maybe you don't now that your kind mascot have a name and a history very curious? Then follow this article to learn a lot of things about this robot.

The first version

The first try to create a mascot emerged in 2007 and came a developer called Dan Morrill 🔗. He tell that opened the Inkscape 🔗 (free software for vectorization of images) and created your own version of robot. The objective it was just personalize the system just for your own team, did not exist any request of the company for a creation of a mascot

That first odd version it was even baptized in honor of your creator: would be the Dandroids.

Arise a new mascot

The idea of having a mascot was maturing and the mission was passed for a professional area. The russian illustrator Irina Blok 🔗, also employee of Google, stayed with the mission to represent the little robot in a way more pleasant.

The main idea of Irina was to represent all gratifying with little traits and in the form more flattened. The drawing also must generate a fast identification for who see. So arise the Bugdroid, the new mascot of Android.

The main inspiration for the traits of the new Bugdroid came those little dolls that illustrate doors of bathrooms to indicate the gender of each door. Tell the legend that the artist was creating in your office desk in Google desktop and seen to the sides of the bathrooms and the identification was immediate: simple, clean, objective.

Want to learn more?

Other curious subject in relation to the Android is that each version always was named in honot of a candy, in alphabet order from version 1.5 to the 9.0.

  • 1.5 - Cupcake
  • 1.6 - Donut
  • 3.0 - Eclair
  • 2.2 - Froyo
  • 2.3 - Gingerbread
  • 3.0 - Honeycomb
  • 4.0 - Ice Cream Sandwich
  • 4.1 - Jelly Bean
  • 4.4 - KitKat
  • 5.0 - Lolipop
  • 6.0 - Marshmallow
  • 7.0 - Nougat
  • 8.0 - Oreo
  • 9.0 - Pie

Unfortunately, the Android Q didn't exist, because the Google decided to put a end of this fun practice and start to use numeration, which gave the origin of Android 10.

Access here the site Android History 🔗 to meet the sequence of "sweet" versions and what each one bring for the Android system.
